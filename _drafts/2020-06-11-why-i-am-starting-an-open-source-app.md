I contributed bits and pieces to various open-source software, but nothing very serious I might say. I also was never a maintainer. 

I use a lot open source in my daily life from programming languages to code editors to ruby gems,  nodejs packages, web frameworks, css frameworks, javascript frameworks. 
It is hard for me to imagine the products I created or creating now without these open-source software. 
When I think about the creators or maintainers of various open-source packages I am truly amazed to see their dedication, their helpful responses, the way they care for the community of users and contributors. For me they are truly the heroes of the modern web. 
In a way these open-source creators remind me of the late 90s when I had my first email address and started using Unix for the first time. I learned a lot from various mailing lists and IRC channels about computers, about how to install and reinstall my OSes, about how to create my first program, about changing config files for some games to make them work. It was a time when we help each other when Google and StackOverflow did not exists. It was real communication supported by a geniune passsion to help others discover the wonders of this new thing we called WWW.

I will start working for a couple of months to a new project: open sourcing the IT Connect app that I developed while I was Innovation Product Owner / Innovation Master at METRO SYSTEMS Romania. The application is a web platform for innovation and idea management. 

The open source application will be developed together with Elena Bogus as Produt Owner and I will be the developer and maintainer. It will actually be a rewritten from scratch because now we have a better and clearer vision about how such platform should be. 

I will like to outline here why I want to do an open-source project and hope that maybe I will inspire others to do the same. 

Before going into that let me give a praise to METRO SYSTEMS Romania. I think accepting to open-source this application is an inspiring decision taken by METRO SYSTEMS Romania and it is along an innovation road they took 6 years ago. In is a great contribution to put there in the world for anyone who wants to use the knowledge that we created there while experimenting with innovation and entreprenership. Just in case you want to have a small taste of some learnings from this journey, Elena wrote a beautiful article about [7 learnings out of building a corporate innovation culture](https://medium.com/metrosystemsro/7-learnings-from-building-a-corporate-innovation-culture-c075dc777117).

Let's start with _WHY_. 


## Why this product?

I am a person with a lot of ideas, I am always working on multiple projects in the same time so I understand the domain. In a way I am scratching my own itch. 

It also matches my experience in the last 5 years while being Innovation Product Owner at METRO SYSTEMS. It is a great opportunity to find a way to code some of the learnings I had while supporting hundreds of people to transform their ideas into real products and launch them. 

I think an innovation management platform will help companies or teams to make their ideas happen faster with greater results. It is very often that I see people saying out loud ideas of products or services that they would like to create but they remain just ideas. Most of the time there no step futher than saying a phrase about them. Having a place where one can submit their idea, where one can gather support or team members, where theer are mentors and sponsorship, where feedback is given is creating more opportunity for anyone to advance their solutions. 

I also strongly belive that innovation is driven by innovation. The more you do the more you will do it. A big part of this is having a place to know what is happening around you, in your organisation. People will get inspired while reading other people ideas. Being transparent about what is happening is a big motivator for more people to contribute. And more people contributing that means more ideas and in the end better ideas. 

I think this process - of making an idea happen - has greater changes to sprakle true innovation if it is digital-first. If an organisation uses a web app to have support and empower their innovation and to make it more appealing to anyone in the company. If there is no digital place to submit ideas, they are often shut down by immediate peers or direct managers. This killing of ideas is removing especially the most innovative ones. But publishing to a digital space allows a wider audience and thus more changes that an idea will live long enough to at least be validated.
