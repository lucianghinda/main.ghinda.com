I'm Lucian Ghinda — a senior product engineer based in Bucharest, Romania. I
have been writing Ruby and Rails since 2007, and in 20+ years in tech I wore
most of the hats a software company has: developer, tech lead, engineering
manager, product owner, and trainer.

That mix was not planned, but I learned to like it. Most of my work sits where
product thinking meets engineering: shipping things people actually use, and
helping the developers around me get better at the craft. If I had to compress
it into one line: **I build products, I teach testing, and I grow the Ruby
community**.

## What I'm focused on now

- **Product engineering in Ruby and Rails** — building features end to end,
  from the product conversation to the deploy.
- **AI-assisted development** — I write a
  [series about using LLMs to write Ruby and Rails code](https://allaboutcoding.ghinda.com/series/ai-code-generation-ruby),
  and at [EuRuKo 2025](https://2025.euruko.org) I ran a workshop called
  _"Don't Let Your AI Guess: Teach It to Test!"_ I think test design is
  becoming more important, not less, now that code is cheap to generate.
- **Test design** — teaching developers how to write fewer tests that cover
  more cases, through [Good Enough Testing](https://goodenoughtesting.com).

## What I make

- **[Short Ruby](https://newsletter.shortruby.com)** — a weekly newsletter I
  have curated every Monday since 2022: a summary of the articles, discussions,
  and news from the Ruby community, read by 6,000+ Ruby developers.
- **[Good Enough Testing](https://goodenoughtesting.com)** — my testing course
  and workshops, teaching systematic test-design techniques (equivalence
  partitioning, boundary values, decision tables, state transitions) through
  live sessions and video. I am an ISTQB certified trainer.
- **[Friendly.rb](https://friendlyrb.com)** — a European Ruby conference I
  co-organize in Bucharest.
- **[Field Notes](https://newsletter.lucianghinda.com)** — short, practical
  notes from my workbench: Ruby, testing, AI-assisted development, building
  products.

## Teaching and community

I have been teaching developers for a long time. In 2013 I founded DevAcademy
(now [Digital Nation](https://digitalnation.ro)), a mentoring program for
junior developers, where I am now a strategic advisor and board member. I
maintain [rubyandrails.info](https://rubyandrails.info), an open source
directory of Ruby learning resources, and I speak and run workshops at Ruby
conferences — most recently [EuRuKo 2024](https://2024.euruko.org) and
[EuRuKo 2025](https://2025.euruko.org).

## Work with me

Here is what I can help you or your company with:

1. **Senior product engineering** — full-time or fractional. I am at my best
   in a team that ships: Ruby, Rails, Hotwire, and lately a lot of
   AI-assisted workflows — because someone on the team has to figure out how
   to use these tools well, and I enjoy being that person.
2. **Team training on test design** — a practical workshop that teaches your
   developers to write the minimum number of tests covering the widest surface
   of your app — because most teams write too many of the wrong tests and too
   few of the right ones.
3. **Reaching Ruby developers** — sponsoring
   [Short Ruby](https://newsletter.shortruby.com/p/sponsorship-packages) puts
   your product or job ad in front of an engaged Ruby audience every Monday.
